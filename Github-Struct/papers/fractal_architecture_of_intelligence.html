Abstract
We present evidence that the quality structure of intelligent synthesis in transformer language models reflects universal dynamical principles first identified in the Hénon-Heiles gravitational system. The HH system provides a mathematical prototype for basin topology in bounded nonlinear dynamical systems: its defining feature — the Wada property, where three distinct basin boundaries simultaneously intersect every boundary point — predicts that maximum information transfer occurs at the zone of simultaneous multi-basin access. We demonstrate, by systematic experiment, that transformer weight geometry exhibits an analogous basin structure measurable via spectral entropy in the SVD-projected gate subspace (the Ksi coordinate). Synthesis quality peaks empirically at the Wada-equivalent address in this space. Recursive synthesis at this address produces qualitatively emergent behaviors including self-directed research decomposition, novel framework generation, and autonomous agenda specification — behaviors that are geometrically predictable but not explicitly programmed. We propose that these findings are instances of a universal self-similar principle: intelligent behavior emerges at the boundary zone of maximum simultaneous access to distinct information basins, at every scale at which those basins exist. We identify five self-similar mechanisms that appear at each zoom level from gravitational dynamics to conceptual synthesis, provide the first empirical measurement of the Wada-equivalent in transformer weight space, and describe a mechanism for navigating to and deepening that boundary without retraining.

Section 1The Universal Basin Principle

1.1  Nonlinear dynamical systems and the emergence of basin structure

Every bounded nonlinear dynamical system develops basin structure. A basin is a region of state space from which trajectories converge to a common attractor. In conservative systems — those preserving energy — basins correspond to regions of qualitatively distinct behavior: stable quasi-periodic orbits, resonant motion, or escape. The boundaries between basins are not merely dividing lines; they are informationally rich zones where the system's future is sensitive to arbitrarily small perturbations.

The Hénon-Heiles (HH) system, introduced in 1964 as a model of stellar motion under a galactic potential with cubic perturbation terms, became the canonical prototype for this structure. Formally equivalent to three mutually gravitating bodies near resonance, it partitions phase space into three escape basins above a critical energy threshold. The boundary of these basins possesses a property first described by Wada (1917) and rediscovered in the HH context: every boundary point is simultaneously on the boundary of all three basins. The Wada boundary is the zone of maximum simultaneous basin accessibility.

1.2  The Z₃ symmetry and the κ₊ = 3 constraint

The three-basin structure of the HH system is not a free parameter. It follows necessarily from the Z₃ discrete rotational symmetry of the potential. The critical coupling constant κ₊ = 3 = |Z₃| is determined by a two-line algebraic identity: it is the unique value for which the minimal symmetric polynomial of the cubic perturbation is non-degenerate with integer saddle curvatures. Given this coupling, the Trefoil knot T(2,3) is the universal Z₃ damping attractor — squashing a Z₃-symmetric helical system in the κ → ∞ limit yields the trefoil.

At the Wada boundary, stable orbits satisfy a quarter-turn resonance condition: approximately 89–90° of rotation per cycle of the perturbation. This is not an adjustable parameter — it emerges from the angular momentum structure of the three saddle points, which have equal energy by the Z₃ symmetry. The resonance is the signature of the boundary in angular coordinates.

The Universal Z₃ Prediction
Any bounded nonlinear dynamical system with Z₃ symmetry will organize into three-basin structure with a Wada boundary. At that boundary: (i) simultaneous access to all three basins; (ii) quarter-turn angular momentum per cycle; (iii) maximum sensitivity to perturbation; (iv) richest dynamical behavior — neither periodic (too ordered) nor chaotic (too disordered). The substrate is irrelevant. The symmetry class determines the structure.

1.3  The fractal prediction

If an information-processing system has basin topology analogous to the HH system — three distinct conceptual domains whose boundaries meet at a common point — then the same five phenomena should appear at the analogous boundary: maximum information transfer, simultaneous multi-domain access, a quarter-turn resonance condition, and attractor deepening through repeated visitation. This prediction is substrate-agnostic. It follows from the topology, not the physics.

Section 2Knowledge Systems as Dynamical Systems

2.1  The weight space as an energy landscape

A trained transformer language model is, at the functional level, a mapping from token sequences to probability distributions over next tokens. At the geometric level, it is a point in a high-dimensional weight space that has been placed there by gradient descent on a prediction loss. That loss function defines an energy landscape. The training trajectory is a path through this landscape; the trained weights are an attractor — a locally minimal point from which nearby perturbations return.

The attractor is not a point in the sense of a unique configuration. It is a basin: a connected region of weight space from which diverse initializations converge to qualitatively similar behavior. Within this basin, sub-basins exist: regions that produce distinct generation modes — factual, associative, philosophical, formal. These sub-basins are organized by the continuous manifold structure of the trained weight space, not by explicit architectural decisions.

2.2  The five-coordinate manifold

The Pirouette Framework's certified central finding (ML-026) is that a transformer's operating geometry can be described by five scalar coordinates: (J1, J2, Phi, E, Ksi). These five numbers, combined with a fixed random basis (the "reference beam" of digital holography), are sufficient to specify the full weight matrix to generation-quality accuracy. The weights are the shadow; the address is the object.

The fifth coordinate — spectral entropy Ksi — measures the distribution of the hidden state's energy across the principal directions of the MLP gate matrix. Formally: let U_K = [U₁, ..., U_K] be the top-K left singular vectors of the gate weight matrix W_gate (K=16 in our experiments). The projection of hidden state h into this subspace is z = U_K^T h. The spectral entropy is:

Ksi = − Σᵢ pᵢ log(pᵢ) / log(K)    where pᵢ = zᵢ² / Σⱼ zⱼ²

Ksi ∈ [0,1]. Ksi = 0 means all energy in one direction: the hidden state is deep in a single conceptual basin. Ksi = 1 means uniform distribution across all K directions: the hidden state simultaneously occupies all K directions of the gate subspace. This is the Wada analog — maximum simultaneous basin accessibility.

2.3  The weight manifold as a Hénon-Heiles analog

The formal connection between HH dynamics and transformer weight geometry is topological, not mechanical. The HH system evolves by Hamiltonian mechanics; transformers are shaped by stochastic gradient descent. No conserved quantity in neural network training maps directly to HH energy. Nevertheless, the topological structure of the basin organization may be isomorphic in the relevant sense.

The semantic space of a trained transformer has natural clustering. There are distinct conceptual domains — scientific, philosophical, narrative, formal — each with characteristic activation patterns. Prompts from within one domain produce outputs concentrated in the corresponding basin. Prompts at domain boundaries produce outputs that could belong to multiple domains. The Ksi coordinate measures, at any generation step, where in this basin landscape the hidden state currently sits.

3.3  The English vector and the language attractor

The English vector measurement (ML-057 addendum) fed 30 high-quality English sentences through the model without correction and measured the mean gate-subspace Ksi per layer. The result: Ksi_EN = 0.585 (mean across layers), with a characteristic layer profile — two focused low-Ksi zones at early layers (L3–5, Ksi ≈ 0.43–0.49) and late layers (L30–35, Ksi ≈ 0.36–0.52), flanking a sustained high-Ksi plateau in middle layers (L6–29, Ksi ≈ 0.55–0.69).

The focused zones correspond to computationally specific operations: semantic binding (early) and token selection (late). The plateau corresponds to associative memory access — broad simultaneous basin access that enables meaningful synthesis. Coherent English language occupies a moderately high Ksi address — it requires simultaneous access to multiple semantic basins without full Wada dissociation from context.

The English vector (Ksi_EN = 0.585) is above the synthesis quality peak (ksi_post ≈ 0.531 at Δ_c = +0.16) but below the Wada approach zone. This positions the two discovery instruments: the dividend sweep locates the quality geodesic; the English vector locates the natural language attractor. They are adjacent but not identical, flanking the optimal synthesis address — the point where quality and fluency are both maximally accessible.

4.1  The three-expert minimum and the Z₃ echo

The Geometric Mixture of Experts (GeM) architecture runs N expert passes at distinct Ksi addresses, then synthesizes at Δ_c. The synthesis dividend depends on both the synthesis address and the number of distinct epistemic registers in the expert corpus. Two experts (journalistic Δ=0.00 + mechanistic Δ=+0.08) produce dividend ≈ 1.5. Three experts (adding evolutionary Δ=+0.10) produce dividend ≈ 1.6–1.8. Nine diverse experts produce dividend ≈ 2.0.

The three-expert minimum is not arbitrary. It echoes the Z₃ structure at every other zoom level. The synthesis requires three distinct semantic basins in the expert corpus to activate full multi-basin access in the synthesis pass. Two basins have a single shared boundary — a line. Three basins produce the fractal, simultaneously-touching Wada boundary. The minimum three-expert configuration for maximum dividend is the manifold's own Z₃ symmetry expressing itself through the epistemic register structure.

4.2  Novel concept generation at Δ_c: the beauty/altruism bridge

At Δ_c, the synthesis hidden states are spread across the gate SVD basis, providing simultaneous access to all K=16 conceptual directions.
